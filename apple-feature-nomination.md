## Detailed Description

Kapselki is a new iPhone arcade game built around a tactile version of the classic Polish backyard bottle-cap race. Players choose a hand-drawn cap character, line up on a chalk route, pull back with one finger, and release to flick the cap toward the finish. The game is designed for short, replayable sessions: a quick match can be played in a few minutes, while Tour, Daily Challenge, and Backyard Champ modes add objectives, progression, and reasons to come back.

The launch version focuses on feel, personality, and a local cultural hook. Each cap has its own stats for power, control, and spin, so the same route can play differently depending on the chosen character. Routes vary by surface and setting, including sidewalk, schoolyard, corridor, carpet, table, grass, sand, bus stop, and workshop boards. Players learn to read chalk lines, manage energy, collect bonuses, avoid off-route penalties, and use risky shortcuts or rebounds when the route allows it.

Kapselki also has a distinctive visual identity: warm, retro-inspired Polish cartoon portraits placed inside worn bottle caps, bright playground colors, chalk lines, simple readable UI, and playful SceneKit physics. It is an offline game with no account, no ads, no tracking, no in-app purchases, and no personal data collection. The goal is to make a small, friendly game that feels immediately familiar to Polish players and fresh to everyone else.

## Why This Is Noteworthy

Kapselki brings a very specific childhood street-game memory to iPhone in a way that fits touch controls naturally. The main interaction is not an abstract button press: it feels like pulling and flicking a real cap. That gives the game a simple hook for new players, while character stats, route styles, energy, objectives, unlocks, and daily challenges create replayability beyond the first race.

The game may be a good fit for App Store editorial themes around indie games, local culture, offline games, family-friendly arcade play, short-session games, tactile touch controls, nostalgic design, or games made with care by small teams.
